About Dharwad – The Cultural Heart of North Karnataka

Located at a height of around 750 meters and spread across seven hills, Dharwad stands as a historic gateway between the Deccan plains and the Western Ghats. With a history going back to the 12th century, the city, along with its twin city Hubballi, forms the second largest urban region in Karnataka.

Fondly known as Vidya Kashi, Dharwad is the education hub of North Karnataka and home to prestigious institutes like IIT Dharwad and IIIT Dharwad. Its calm and pleasant climate has always encouraged creativity in music, literature, and learning.

A true cradle of Hindustani classical music, this city has given the country legendary artists such as Pt. Bhimsen Joshi, Gangubai Hangal, Sawai Gandharva, Kumar Gandharva, Mallikarjun Mansur, and Basavaraj Rajaguru. In the field of literature, it is the homeland of Jnanpith awardees D. R. Bendre, V. K. Gokak, and Girish Karnad.

Popularly called Pedha Nagari because of its world-famous milk pedhas, Dharwad has for generations welcomed travellers passing between the mountains and the plains. The name comes from the Sanskrit word “Dwarawata”, which means “a gateway town”.

Summers here are moderate with temperatures going up to around 35°C, winters are cool at about 15°C, and the monsoon rains that arrive between June and September nourish the region’s red soil. Dharwad is truly a place where culture, education, music, and food live together in perfect harmony.
